POE Tokenomics

The POE token represents a virtual share of the business of the charging infrastructure project. Token holders are entitled to receive income from the network of charging stations (CS) funded by the proceeds from token sales.

The project is funded by selling the POE token, as well as by allocating a portion of the net profit to business development.

Token Distribution and Sale

All issued tokens are either sold or distributed according to the following system:

    Private Sale - private sale to investors on special terms.

    App Sale / Stock - sale of tokens on the exchange or through a mini app to any user at a fixed price with restrictions described in the application.

    Net Tasks - utilization of tokens as a means of payment for work on the development of the charging station network. Tasks and vacancies will be available in the application. This is one of the ways to implement token functionality - as a payment method in the electric transportation.

    Airdrop - distribution of tokens as bonuses to the community and new project participants.

    Development - distribution of tokens for work, development, marketing, and management of the charging station network. Operational expenses.

    Founders - distribution of tokens to the team.

Funds from the sale of tokens through App Sale / Stock Sale / Private Sale are allocated to the installation of charging stations.

Installation of Charging Stations

The team organizes analysis, site search, power supply, selection and purchase of charging stations, as well as their installation and setup. Assistance from the community at any stage of the work is welcomed and encouraged.

After installation, the charging station is connected to the general network.

Profitability of Charging Stations and POE Yield

Net profit = Revenue from charging stations minus the cost: electricity, location rental, maintenance, and other expenses.

Net profit from the charging station is divided into the following components:

Development - the network's own fund for purchasing new charging stations, as well as for paying for development, marketing, and network management. Operational expenses.

The network sets aside part of the profit for purchasing new charging stations in addition to installing them by issuing new tokens, thus engaging in independent self-expansion and increasing business profits. The percentage of net profit for business development can be increased or decreased based on community voting results. Similarly, the type and location of charging station installations can be changed in the same manner.

Reserve fund - a fund for unforeseen expenses, losses, or token buybacks.

Team - distribution of profits to the team.

Dividends - a portion of the net profit from the charging station network distributed among token holders who keep them in the staking.

Dividends distribution is implemented through Staking.

Distribution in staking occurs as follows: POE is redeemed from the exchange for the amount of dividends, then the received POE are sent to staking.

This approach to income distribution is convenient for use and reinvestment and also stimulates the systematic growth of the exchange rate.

System maintenance, efficiency improvement

The following processes help effectively manage the network:

Monitoring of stations and their profitability.

Support, repair, and maintenance of stations, including using POE tokens.

Evaluation of the effectiveness of current locations and assessment of potential optimization of sites.

Search for new locations, potential assessment of their profitability, and suitable types of charging stations, including with the help of the community.

Relocation of stations from inefficient locations.

Testing hypotheses.

Automation of the process of accepting stations into the network (evaluation of performance, etc.) through governance.
